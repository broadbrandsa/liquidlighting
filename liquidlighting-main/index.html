EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Liquid Lighting is at a moment of real opportunity.

The business already has strong credibility in the lighting industry, trusted relationships, and deep technical expertise. What's missing is a digital platform that matches that reputation, one that supports both retail growth and professional specification at scale.

What Success Looks Like +

Six months after go-live, Liquid Lighting has a dependable retail revenue channel that operates alongside its existing commercial business, not in competition with it.

Retail customers can browse, understand, and purchase products with confidence. Professional customers can request quotes, access specifications, and move projects forward without friction. Internally, teams are aligned around a single platform that supports sales, service, and education.

Success is not defined by features alone, but by how naturally the platform fits into day-to-day operations and customer journeys.

Measurable Impact +

While exact performance targets will be refined post-launch, early success is expected to show up in clear, measurable ways.

These include faster quote turnaround times, improved retail conversion rates, reduced manual handovers between teams, and better visibility across customers, products, and performance.

Together, these indicators provide Liquid Lighting with early validation that the platform is delivering both commercial and operational value.

Internal Efficiency +

The platform is designed to reduce complexity, not add to it.

Sales, support, and operations teams gain access to shared customer, quote, and order information, reducing duplication and follow-ups. Automated workflows handle common requests, while structured data replaces ad-hoc spreadsheets and inbox searches.

This allows teams to spend less time chasing information and more time serving customers and advancing projects.

Market Positioning +

Beyond revenue and efficiency, the platform strengthens Liquid Lighting‚Äôs position in the market.

Through structured content, professional tools, and consistent digital experiences, Liquid Lighting presents itself as an authority in lighting, not just a supplier of products.

Customers and professionals experience clarity, confidence, and speed. Over time, this builds trust, repeat engagement, and long-term brand leadership across South Africa‚Äôs lighting industry.

Understanding Requirements
Based on the RFQ, Liquid Lighting needs a platform that does several things at once:
  • Support retail e-commerce and generate new revenue quickly.
  • Serve architects, designers, contractors, and internal teams with proper tools.
  • Automate processes that are currently manual or fragmented.
  • Act as an information and education hub for the industry.
  • Be easy to manage and evolve after handover.

This proposal has been structured specifically to meet those needs, without overengineering complexity.

STRATEGY

We propose a phased digital ecosystem built on Shopify and supported by a custom integration and CRM layer.

Liquid Lighting operates at the intersection of technical specification, design credibility, and trust.

This approach supports complex products, mixed customer types, and professional decision-making without forcing everything into a retail-only model.

It allows retail growth without compromising the needs of architects, designers, and contractors, while positioning Liquid Lighting as a long-term industry authority.
